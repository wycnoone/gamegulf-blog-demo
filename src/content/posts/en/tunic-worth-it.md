## What kind of game is TUNIC, really?

**TUNIC is an isometric action-adventure built around exploration, manual-page discovery, and hidden-rule puzzles.** The store pitch names shady forests, ruins, catacombs, colossal bosses, strange items, and a reconstructed instruction manual; those are not side details, they are the actual buying hook.

1. **Exploration first:** the world is interconnected and secret-heavy, closer in buying appeal to a compact Zelda-style mystery box than to a pure combat gauntlet.
2. **Manual-page puzzles:** collecting illustrated pages is part tutorial, part map, part riddle layer; players who like decoding UI clues get more value than players who want direct quest markers.
3. **Combat friction:** dodge, block, parry, and boss learning matter, so the cute fox presentation should not be mistaken for a frictionless cozy game.

Compared with something like **INSIDE**, which GameGulf lists nearby as another highly rated puzzle-adventure, TUNIC is less linear and more interested in secrets, route reading, and player inference.
