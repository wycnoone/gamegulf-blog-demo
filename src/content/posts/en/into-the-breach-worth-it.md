## What kind of game is Into the Breach, really?

**Into the Breach** is a turn-based tactics puzzle from **Subset Games**, the studio behind **FTL**, built around tiny maps, three-mech squads, and enemies that show their attacks one turn ahead. The fun is not loot sprawl; it is pushing Vek, shielding civilian buildings, choosing pilots and weapons, and solving each board like a combat puzzle.

Compared with broader strategy games, this is closer to chess problems with roguelite campaign structure: islands reset, squads change, and failure sends help back through time rather than asking you to grind levels. That makes it best for players who enjoy clean information, hard tradeoffs, and short missions that still punish sloppy positioning.

## How does Into the Breach run on Nintendo Switch?

**Into the Breach** is a strong Switch fit because its 2D pixel art, grid UI, and turn-based input do not depend on twitch timing or heavy effects. Handheld play is especially natural: the board is small, the threat markers are readable, and each mission can be handled in a short session.

Docked mode mostly improves comfort rather than changing the experience. Common player reports focus more on the puzzle difficulty and undo-limited decisions than on performance problems, which is exactly what you want from a portable tactics port.
