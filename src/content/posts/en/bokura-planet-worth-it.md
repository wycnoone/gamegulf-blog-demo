## What kind of game is BOKURA: planet, really?

**BOKURA: planet** is a **two-player puzzle adventure** from **tokoronyori**, the creator behind **BOKURA**, and the brief is much more specific than the current genre tags. Each player is on a separate machine, both players must talk through what they see, and the game explicitly says you need your own outside communication method such as **voice chat**.

Two details matter more than generic adjectives. First, the game adds a **Push** action that can help or sabotage your partner, so the co-op is not just peaceful switch-flipping. Second, the campaign uses **hidden objectives**, which means you are cooperating while each player also holds private information. If that tension sounds fun, the pitch works; if you want friction-free couch co-op, this is a shakier fit.

## How does BOKURA: planet run on NS2?

**The useful expectation is format, not benchmark chasing.** The brief supports a **controller-first, split-information co-op** read, and the important usability question is whether you can comfortably play on **two machines** while staying in sync over voice chat.
