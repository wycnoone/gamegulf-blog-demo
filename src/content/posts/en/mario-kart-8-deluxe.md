## Quick verdict

**Mario Kart 8 Deluxe is still one of Nintendo Switch's most rational purchases in 2026 — if people actually sit down to play with you.** It solves a boring-but-real problem: *what do we play together that nobody needs a tutorial for?* If your Switch life is mostly solitary, the same game becomes easier to skip or to buy only on sale.

## What kind of game is Mario Kart 8 Deluxe, really?

Mario Kart 8 Deluxe is **arcade kart racing** built around **drift boosts, chaotic items, and short races** that reset emotional stakes every three laps. The Deluxe package on Switch adds **inkling characters, a proper battle mode refresh, and two-item holding in races** compared with the original Wii U release.

1. **Multiplayer-first design** — Local split-screen and wireless play stay the killer feature.
2. **Online longevity** — Tournaments and matchmaking keep it relevant years after purchase.
3. **Optional depth** — Time trials and vehicle customization reward enthusiasts without scaring newcomers.

The **Booster Course Pass** (sold separately) expands the track roster across multiple waves; if you want "everything Nintendo has shipped for this generation of MK8," plan for that add-on cost.

## How does it run on Nintendo Switch?

**Mario Kart 8 Deluxe targets 60 fps during actual races** on Switch, which matters because responsiveness is the whole point.

- **Docked:** Typically 1080p at 60 fps in races
- **Handheld:** 720p at 60 fps in races — strong fit for portable multiplayer
- **Battle modes:** May differ slightly from race scenarios but remain smooth for party use

This is one of the better-performing major first-party titles on the hardware, and that stability is part of why it stays in rotation.

## The blue shell is a feature, not a bug

**Mario Kart survives because chaos keeps outcomes uncertain.** The same randomness that makes players swear also makes rematches inevitable — which is exactly what a party game needs.

**If you have people to play with, Mario Kart 8 Deluxe is less a gamble than most purchases.** Confirm your live regional price once, add the Booster Pass to your mental budget if you care about the full track list, and you are done overthinking it.
