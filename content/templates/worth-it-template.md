## Quick verdict

Open with a bold, direct answer in 2 paragraphs:
- Paragraph 1: Yes/no + the key condition (genre fit, time, price).
- Paragraph 2: Reframe the question — "the real question is not X, it is Y."

Keep it under 80 words total.

## How much does [Game Title] cost on Switch right now?

The pricing section comes EARLY because it is the primary reason most readers arrive.

- Lead with the lowest regional price (EUR equivalent)
- List 2–3 notable regional data points
- Explain why cross-region comparison matters for this game
- One natural GameGulf mention when citing price data

## What kind of game is [Game Title], really?

For readers who have never played it. Structure as a numbered list:
1. Core mechanic / genre identity
2. Secondary loop or structure
3. The "vibe" — what makes it feel different

Then one paragraph connecting these elements into the core appeal.

## How does it run on Switch?

Practical, honest performance summary:
- Frame rate and stability
- Load times
- Handheld vs docked trade-offs
- Content parity with other platforms
- Double-dip assessment for existing owners

## Buy now if

5 bullet points. Each one should describe a player type or situation, not just a feature.

## Wait or skip if

4 bullet points. Be honest about friction points. End with constructive advice (watch gameplay, set alert, etc) rather than just "don't buy."

## [A thematic closing section]

Name this section after the game's core tension — e.g. "The 100-hour question" for long RPGs, "The price-per-session math" for multiplayer games.

Two short paragraphs with bold openers:
- **If [positive framing], buy it.** + why the payoff is worth it.
- **If [negative framing], it probably is [a warning].** + no shame in knowing your taste.

Do NOT include a "Best next move on GameGulf" section — the page template renders structured CTA cards automatically.
